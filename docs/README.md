# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management documentation suite. This README provides an overview of our methodology and links to each process guide so new teammates can quickly orient themselves and navigate to the guidance they need.

## Overview

OctoAcme follows a lightweight, iterative project management framework designed to deliver customer value quickly while maintaining quality and transparency. Our approach is grounded in five core principles: customer-first prioritization, iterative delivery of small testable increments, clear ownership with a named Project Manager (PM) and Product Lead on every project, data-informed decision-making, and a culture of psychological safety that encourages open feedback. Projects move through five lifecycle phases — **Initiation**, **Planning**, **Execution**, **Release**, and **Retrospective** — each with defined deliverables, checklists, and decision gates to keep work moving without unnecessary overhead.

Three primary personas drive delivery: the **Project Manager** coordinates schedules, risk, and cross-team communication; the **Product Manager** owns the product vision, prioritizes the backlog, and measures outcomes; and **Developers** design, build, and test the software. These roles collaborate around a shared set of key artifacts — a Project One-pager, Roadmap and Release Plan, Sprint Backlog with Acceptance Criteria, Risk Register, and Retrospective notes. Communication is structured around a weekly PM–PdM alignment sync, twice-weekly team standups, monthly stakeholder updates, and ad-hoc escalation channels when blockers arise.

Execution quality is maintained through a disciplined PR workflow (small pull requests, CI-enforced tests and linting, mandatory code review), layered testing practices (unit, integration, end-to-end smoke tests, and security scanning), and a three-level blocker escalation path from team triage through PM to sponsor-level resolution. Risks are tracked in a living Risk Register and reviewed every week, while release safety is ensured via staging smoke tests, rollback plans, and post-deploy verifications before any production announcement.

Continuous improvement is built into the cadence: after every sprint, release, or significant incident the team holds a structured retrospective to capture what went well, what needs improvement, and two to three prioritized action items with clear owners and due dates. Action items feed back into the project backlog and are reviewed in the weekly PM sync, creating a closed feedback loop that lets OctoAcme evolve its processes incrementally and measurably over time.
